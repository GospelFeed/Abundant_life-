⚠️Warnings from 2 Peter About False New Testament Churches and Pastors Who Do Not Fully Follow Paul's Teachings but Instead Twist the Truth for Their Own Benefit and Deceive Believers

False teachers deceive believers by selectively quoting Scripture for their own benefit while ignoring passages that correct or challenge them. The Apostle Paul taught that those who preach the gospel have the right to receive support (1 Corinthians 9:13–14). However, Paul also chose, on many occasions, to support himself through his own work so that he would not place a financial burden on the churches or give anyone an opportunity to question his motives (Acts 20:33–35; 1 Thessalonians 2:9; 2 Thessalonians 3:7–10). Peter warned that false teachers would twist the Scriptures, including Paul's writings, to their own destruction and would lead others astray (2 Peter 3:15–16). Rather than teaching the whole counsel of God, they may emphasize only those verses that benefit them while neglecting passages that call for humility, hard work, self-sacrifice, and integrity. Believers are therefore encouraged to examine all teaching carefully in light of the whole of Scripture and to follow the example of the apostles.
